How We Calculate Your Program Matches
Our Weighting Methodology

The TECH COMPASS uses a weighted scoring system developed by:

  • Program Directors - Each program director identified the key skills, work environments, and personality traits that lead to success in their field
  • Advisory Committees - Local employers validated what they look for when hiring graduates
  • Curriculum Analysis - Course outlines were reviewed to identify core competencies
  • Labor Market Data - Current industry demands and future job growth projections
The 5-Point Scale
Weight Meaning Definition Example
5 Essential / Core This skill is taught in the first semester and used daily "Fix problems" → Cybersecurity (CYB)
4 Strong Match Emphasized throughout the program, used weekly "Hands-on practice" → Welding (WELD)
3 Good Match Important but not the primary focus "Accuracy" → Pharmacy (PHARM)
2 Moderate Match Supporting skill, used occasionally "Documentation" → Medical Assisting (MA)
1 Minor Match Related but not a primary focus "Creativity" → Automotive (COL)
Your Score Calculation

Total Score = Sum of all weights from your selected answers

Maximum possible: 100 points (20 questions × 5 points). Your match percentage shows how closely you align with each program's ideal student profile.
